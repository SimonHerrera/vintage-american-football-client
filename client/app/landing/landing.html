Vintage American Football

What is Vintage American Football...

Vintage football is for historians and enthusiasts – those of us who are drawn to the simplicity of the game in its early years.

If you love the aesthetics of football in the 1920s – including the unique jersey designs, leather helmets, and fat football – you’ll love participating in vintage football, which is part athletic pursuit, and part historical exploration.

Basic Rules We Use...

To play 1920's football you need to start by using the same equipment. We use a replica Melon football that is much wider and harder to throw than a modern day football.

We use a field that is has no hash marks and has the goal posts set on the goalline.

The offensive formations and the ball promotes "Straight Football", running the ball as they did in the 1920's.

Special rules promote team attempting drop kicks for field goals and extra point

1920's rule example - If ball is thrown into the end zone it is considered a touchback and goes to the defense.

The goals of the game...

To entertain and educate communities by playing games that have the look and feel of a 1920s football game.

To create a game that for the players is safe and uses similar equipment, rules and strategies from 1920's football.

Goals and resources on this site...

- Complete list of all leagues, teams and players who are playing by our Vintage American Football rules.

- Information on how to organize your own game using our rules and equipment with your own players or maybe even ours.

- Read about the history of pro football.

- Links to articles and photo gallaries from previous Vintage American Football games.

Our first game...

On August 23rd, 2015 the first Vintage American Football game was played at Douglas Park in Rock Island Illinois.

The same site also hosted the very first game involving a team from the newly formed NFL (95 years earlier) in 1920.
